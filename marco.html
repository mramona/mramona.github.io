Research Interests &
Short Bio


My research interests are in the area of wireless sensor networks. For my PhD project I am particularly interested in analyzing and predicting the impact of the environment on WSN connectivity.
Traces used in my reasearch work are from in-field experimental campaigns performed on Mount Bondone, near Trento. Traces are collected using TRIDENT, a tool developed in our group for the execution of communication tests without the need for a tethered infrastructure.

I am a member of the D3S research group and work under the supervision of Prof. Gian Pietro Picco.

In 2002 I have received a B.Sc. degree in Computer Science from the Faculty of Automation and Computer Science at Technical University of Cluj-Napoca, Romania and in 2003 I have completed my M.Sc. in Information Systems at the same faculty. From 2002 to 2010 I've been with the Computer Science Department at Technical University of Cluj-Napoca.
